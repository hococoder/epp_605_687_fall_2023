Week 2 - Introduction to Combine

Overview

In the next few modules, I'm going to go over exactly what Combine is, how it can be used to flow data through your app, and how it's used in various frameworks in macOS.

Combine is Apple's response to the reactive programming craze that's been popular with frameworks like React. Combine works on asynchronous streams of information in your program. Using reactive programming with user interfaces is probably the example most people are familiar with, but that's not the only place you can use it. If you have publishers of information, and subscribers who want to listen for that data - possibly with operations on that data in between - you may have a great candidate to use Combine.

Combine uses the concept of backpressure to control the flow of data. What exactly does that mean? Well, think about a water hose, with a sprayer at the end. When you turn on the water at the spigot, water fills the hose, but doesn't come out of the sprayer. When the user squeezes the handle - asking for water - it comes out until they don't want anymore, and they handle gets released.

Here, the spigot is the Publisher of the data - which is water in this example - and the sprayer is the Subscriber of the data. We don't have an operator here that changes the water, but if we hooked up a plant feeder bottle before the sprayer, that would be a great representation of an operator, turning the water into nutritious water for the plants.

Let's look at these components in a slightly different way, because eventually these concepts have to be represented in code. We'll start with Publishers in the next module.
